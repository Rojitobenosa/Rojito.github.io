Welcome

This website aims to raise awareness about internet addiction, its causes, effects, and how to manage it effectively.

About Internet Addiction

Internet addiction is the excessive use of the internet that leads to negative impacts on an individual's daily life, mental health, and social relationships. It can manifest as compulsive gaming, social media engagement, or excessive browsing.

Causes of Internet Addiction

  • Escapism: Using the internet to escape from real-life problems or stress.
  • Instant Gratification: The quick rewards from social media likes, online games, and videos can lead to addiction.
  • Social Pressure: Feeling the need to stay connected or up to date with online trends.
  • Lack of Offline Activities: Limited access to other hobbies or social interactions can drive individuals to excessive internet use.

Effects of Internet Addiction

Internet addiction can have various physical, emotional, and social effects, including:

  • Sleep disorders due to late-night screen time.
  • Increased stress, anxiety, and depression.
  • Strained relationships with family and friends.
  • Poor academic or work performance due to distraction.

Tips for Managing Internet Addiction

1. Set Time Limits: Use apps or built-in features to limit your screen time and stay aware of your usage.

2. Engage in Offline Activities: Find hobbies and activities like reading, exercising, or spending time with friends and family that don't involve the internet.

3. Practice Mindfulness: Be conscious of when and why you're going online, and avoid mindless scrolling or browsing.

4. Seek Help if Necessary: If you feel that your internet use is out of control, don't hesitate to seek professional help or talk to someone you trust.
